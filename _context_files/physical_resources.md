# Physical Resources

Usage notes:
1. The six groupings are for organizational purposes only; they have no functional effect.
2. "Extractable" means that the item exists in natural bodies or natural strata (at least somewhere) and may be a direct target of extraction (possibly in addition to other sourcing).
3. All not marked as extractable must be sourced entirely from refining, manufacturing, and/or agriculture.
4. All resources (including catchall terms) are abstracted as singular commodities with a common price by weight.
5. Elemental compositions are only for reference. Manufacturing inputs should be considered as fungible to a large degree (and where resource composition isn't explicit). This is true also for materials where it makes sense (e.g., concrete).

## Materials

- **Steel** Carbon steel, alloy steel, stainless steel.
- **Aluminum** Refined Al metal and alloys.
- **Industrial Metals** Cu, Ti, Zn, Ni, Sn, Pb, W, Mo, Co, Mn, Cr, etc.; also includes Ag.
- **Precious Metals** Au, Pt, Pd, Rh, Ru, Ir, Os (excludes Ag).
- **Rare Earths** REE oxides, metals, alloys, permanent-magnet stock.
- **Concrete** Portland cement, geopolymer, sintered-regolith binder.
- **Glass/Ceramics** Flat glass, fiber glass, refractory and technical ceramics.
- **Semiconductor Materials** High-purity Si, GaAs, SiC, compound semiconductors.
- **Polymers** Thermoplastics, thermosets, elastomers, synthetic fibers.
- **Composites** CFRP, GFRP, metal-matrix composites, aramid laminates.
- **Technical Textiles** — High-performance fabrics and associated finished products; aramid, UHMWPE, carbon fiber cloth, PTFE membranes, glass fiber textiles, multilayer insulation, filtration media, spacesuits; ref. production-weighted blend. C 65, O 21, H 7, N 3, F 1.5, Si 1, other (Ca, Al, B, Mg, Fe, Na, S, Cl) 1.5.
- **Bulk Chemicals** Commodity chemicals: acids, bases, oxidizers, solvents, industrial gases (Cl₂, HF, etc.).
- **Fine Chemicals** Specialty chemicals, catalysts, coatings, adhesives, electronic-grade reagents.
- **Industrial Waste** Scrap metal, slag, fly ash, spent catalysts, recyclable mixed waste.
- **Radioactive Waste** Spent fission fuel, activated structural material, etc. (for modeling, consider this a mass-weighted average of intermediate- and high-level waste).
