## Simplifications and Key Assumptions

**Mass balance methodology.** All refining operations satisfy sum-of-inputs = sum-of-outputs = mass\_flow, within rounding. For chemical conversions I used stoichiometric quantities per the schema. For separation/fabrication operations (oil refining, iron-nickel refining, recycling) I used representative industrial yields and compositions from the physical\_resources definitions.

**Electricity represents direct electrical demand only.** For operations where the primary energy comes from a chemical input (IRON\_ORE\_SMELTING uses coke combustion; BASIC\_O2\_STEELMAKING uses exothermic C/Si oxidation), the electricity field captures only auxiliary electrical loads (blowers, charging, casting). The chemical energy is carried implicitly in the CARBON or IRON inputs. Operations where electricity IS the primary energy source (ALUMINIUM\_REFINING at \(-2.80\) MW, ELECTRIC\_STEELMAKING at \(-0.40\) MW, REGOLITH\_PROCESSING at \(-5.00\) MW) show correspondingly larger values.

**BASIC\_O2\_STEELMAKING and ELECTRIC\_STEELMAKING share the same mass balance** but differ in electricity and efficiency. Both convert pig iron to steel with oxygen refining; the chemical transformation is identical, only the heat source differs (exothermic oxidation vs. electric arc). A more detailed model could differentiate scrap ratios.

**SOLAR\_STEELMAKING outputs CARBON\_MONOXIDE** rather than CO₂, since the carbothermal reduction product (CO from Fe₂O₃ + 3C → 2Fe + 3CO) exits the solar furnace without a secondary combustion step. This CO is a valuable syngas feedstock for downstream Fischer-Tropsch or methanol synthesis.

**INDUST\_RECYC\_INCINERATION outputs REGOLITH** for the vitrified slag residue rather than INDUST\_WASTE, to avoid having the same resource as both input and output. Vitrified slag is compositionally similar to regolith (bulk silicate aggregate), and the description notes it is "usable as aggregate."

**MUNICIPAL\_RECYC\_INCINERATION is net electricity-positive** (+0.24 MW) from waste-to-energy steam turbine recovery, offsetting sorting/processing loads.

**PRECIOUS\_METALS\_REFINING and DEUTERIUM\_PURIFICATION** are normalized to 1 kg/h mass\_flow (1e-3 t/h) per the schema instruction for very high value-to-weight products. Precious metals output is 0.87 mg/h per kg of concentrate (matching the 870 ppm ore grade). Deuterium is modeled as the net reaction D₂O → D₂ + ½O₂ with the massive separation energy (∼500 kWh/kg D₂) captured in the electricity field.

**Fuel reprocessing operations include waste immobilization.** URANIUM\_FUEL\_REPROCESSING and THORIUM\_FUEL\_REPROCESSING consume INDUST\_MINERALS as the cementation/vitrification matrix for RADIOACTIVE\_WASTE output, and INDUST\_CHEMICALS for fluorination of recovered uranium to UF₆ (matching the URANIUM resource definition). The INDUST\_MINERALS input is the largest by mass, reflecting that vitrified HLW is ∼96.5% glass/cement matrix by the RADIOACTIVE\_WASTE resource definition (3.5% actinides/FP).

**Oil refining omits the Claus process O₂/H₂O stoichiometry** (∼0.007 t/h O₂ consumed, ∼0.008 t/h H₂O produced) as negligible relative to 1 t/h mass flow, absorbing the discrepancy into INDUST\_WASTE. METHANE output represents refinery light ends (LPG, fuel gas). CARBON represents petroleum coke.

**Industrial metals refining is heavily aggregated** — a composite of Cu, Ni, Zn, Cr, Mn, Ti, and other base-metal smelting/refining processes. The CARBON input covers carbothermal reduction of oxide-hosted metals (Cr, Mn). The CARBON\_MONOXIDE output comes from these reduction reactions and is available as downstream syngas feedstock.

## Feedback on Framework

A few observations that might improve downstream work:

**Process heat tracking.** The current schema tracks electricity but not process heat explicitly. Operations like IRON\_ORE\_SMELTING and OIL\_REFINING get most of their energy from burning chemical inputs (coke, refinery gas), which is reflected in the mass balance but not in the electricity field. If the simulation later needs to track total energy demand or CO₂ intensity, a separate "process heat" field or an explicit "fuel burned for heat" stream might be useful.

**Scrap recycling pathway.** ELECTRIC\_STEELMAKING currently takes pig iron as input (same mass balance as BOS). In practice, EAF is the dominant scrap-recycling route. You might want a separate EAF-scrap variant that takes INDUST\_WASTE or a "SCRAP\_STEEL" resource as input, with lower CARBON/OXYGEN requirements and no INDUST\_MINERALS flux.

**Inert-anode aluminium smelting.** The current ALUMINIUM\_REFINING models carbon-anode Hall-Héroult (consuming CARBON, producing CO₂). A forward-looking inert-anode variant would consume no carbon, produce O₂ instead of CO₂, and be significantly more relevant for off-Earth production. This could be a separate operation sharing the same module.

**Regolith processing selectivity.** The current model fully reduces regolith (extracting all O₂). In practice, partial reduction targeting only FeO or ilmenite is more energy-efficient. You might want a "selective regolith reduction" variant with lower electricity but only OXYGEN and IRON outputs (no ALUMINIUM).
