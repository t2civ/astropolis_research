Lepidolite and petalite fractions contribute the F, Rb, and Cs.

The weight fraction assigned to the Li-pegmatite component is ~6% of the total blend. Current lithium hard-rock concentrate production is roughly 2% of total industrial metal concentrate mass (approximately 4 Mt spodumene concentrate out of ~200 Mt total Cu-Mn-Cr-Zn-Ni concentrates globally). I applied a forward-weighting factor of ~3× to account for projected lithium demand growth through the 2030s–2040s (IEA Global Critical Minerals Outlook 2024 projects 3–4× demand increase by 2040 under stated-policy scenarios). The remaining 94% of the blend retains the original composition.

Brine-derived lithium (currently about half of global Li supply and growing) is handled separately in the simulation as a refined product from water/brine processing, consistent with the document's treatment of aqueous extraction pathways. This means the Industrial Metal Ores entry captures only hard-rock lithium mining; the simulation must also model Li recovery from brines if total Li supply is to be represented accurately.
